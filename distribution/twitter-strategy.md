## Engagement Tactics

### Quote Tweets (QRTs)
- When a major OSINT account posts raw intelligence, QRT with your analysis and dashboard link
- Format: "[Your analysis of what this means] — tracking this live on war-room.live"
- Target: 3-5 QRTs per day of high-visibility posts
- Always add value. Never QRT just to say "interesting" — add context, data, or a counter-take

### Reply Strategy
- Reply to top OSINT/defense accounts within 15 minutes of their posts (early replies get visibility)
- Provide data points from the dashboard that complement their reporting
- Format: "Adding context: [data point from dashboard]. Source: [source]. More at war-room.live"
- Never self-promote without adding substantive information first

### Engagement Triggers
- When other accounts post unverified claims: reply with verification status from your sourcing
- When market accounts discuss oil/gold moves: reply connecting it to specific conflict events
- When policy accounts discuss diplomacy: reply with Kahn Scale context
- When anyone asks "what's happening in Iran?": reply with your latest Daily Briefing thread link

### Community Building
- Create and run weekly polls: "What's your Kahn Scale assessment this week?" with options
- Ask open questions: "What's the most underreported aspect of this conflict right now?"
- Host Twitter Spaces (Friday evenings, 30-45 min) with a specific topic each week
- Acknowledge and retweet community members who share good analysis

## Growth Milestones & Tactics

### Stage 1: 0 to 500 Followers (Weeks 1-4)

**Goal:** Establish credibility and find core audience.

**Tactics:**
- Post 5-8 tweets per day minimum (including replies)
- Publish the Daily Briefing thread every single day without exception
- Reply to every major OSINT post within 15 minutes with substantive data
- Follow 200-300 accounts in the OSINT/defense/geopolitics space
- Pin the dashboard announcement tweet
- Post 2-3 screenshots of the dashboard per day showing real-time data
- Join every relevant Twitter Space as a listener; request to speak when you have data to add
- DM 10-15 accounts per day introducing the dashboard (not spammy — genuine, personalized)

**Key metric:** Replies that get 5+ likes (indicates the audience finds the data valuable).

**Content ratio:** 40% original threads, 30% replies/engagement, 20% QRTs, 10% dashboard promo.

### Stage 2: 500 to 2,000 Followers (Weeks 5-12)

**Goal:** Build authority and consistent engagement.

**Tactics:**
- Maintain daily thread cadence — never miss a day
- Launch the Market Impact thread (Thursdays) and Escalation Watch thread (daily evening)
- Start weekly Twitter Spaces with a specific topic each week
- Collaborate: co-threads with 2-3 OSINT accounts (they provide intel, you provide dashboard data)
- Cross-promote on LinkedIn (post the daily briefing as an article, link to X)
- Share "how I built this" behind-the-scenes content (people love seeing the tech)
- Get quoted/cited by at least 2 journalists or larger accounts (pitch them directly)
- Use X Analytics to identify which content types perform best; double down

**Key metric:** Thread impressions consistently above 10K. Profile visits above 500/week.

**Content ratio:** 35% original threads, 25% replies/engagement, 20% QRTs, 10% Spaces/collabs, 10% promo.

### Stage 3: 2,000 to 5,000 Followers (Weeks 13-26)

**Goal:** Scale reach and convert followers to newsletter/dashboard users.

**Tactics:**
- Add the newsletter CTA to every thread closer: "Daily on X, weekly deep dive in your inbox: [link]"
- Launch "War Room Weekly Top 10" — a curated list of the best OSINT tweets of the week (tags those accounts, they retweet)
- Guest on 2+ podcasts per month discussing the conflict (mention X handle and dashboard)
- Create a signature visual format: a branded card template for daily stats (Canva or Figma)
- Run occasional "prediction threads" with follow-up accountability: "Last week I said X, here's what happened"
- Publish a monthly "State of the Conflict" mega-thread (20+ tweets, comprehensive)
- Consider X Premium for longer posts and priority ranking in replies
- Build a list on X: "War Room Sources" — curated accounts. People will follow your list and discover you

**Key metric:** Follower growth rate above 200/week. Daily Briefing thread consistently 25K+ impressions. Newsletter signups from X above 50/week.

**Content ratio:** 30% original threads, 25% replies/engagement, 15% QRTs, 15% cross-promo (newsletter, Spaces, collabs), 15% community building (polls, lists, recognition).

## Content Rules

1. **Never post unverified information.** Every claim must have a source. This is the core differentiator from other X accounts. When uncertain, say "Unverified report from [source] — tracking for confirmation."
2. **Always link to the dashboard.** Every thread should end with a war-room.live link. Not every tweet, but every thread.
3. **Data over opinion.** Lead with numbers, sources, and verified facts. Analysis comes after data. Never editorialize about who is "right" — stay analytical.
4. **Speed matters.** When something breaks, be in the first 10 accounts to post about it with verified data. Speed + accuracy = authority.
5. **Visual content performs 2-3x better.** Include dashboard screenshots, map screenshots, or data cards in at least 50% of tweets.
6. **Thread length: 4-7 tweets.** Longer than 7 loses readers. Shorter than 4 doesn't justify the thread format — just tweet it.
7. **No partisan framing.** The dashboard and account are intelligence tools, not political commentary. Analyze all sides equally.
