Summary

On February 28, 2026, the United States launched a coordinated military strike campaign against Iran's nuclear infrastructure, killing Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei in the opening salvo. The campaign, described by President Trump as lasting "approximately four weeks," has escalated into a full regional war involving Israel, Lebanon (Hezbollah), Yemen (Houthis), and Iraqi PMF militias.

By Day 5, the Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed, VLCC tanker rates have hit an all-time record of $423,000/day, and the conflict has expanded to 5 theaters: the Persian Gulf, Eastern Mediterranean, Indian Ocean, Lebanon, and Yemen.
